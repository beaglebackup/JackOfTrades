Bird Mite

(Ornithonyssus sylviarum)


BACKGROUND INFORMATION. Mites are closely related to spiders and typically feed on a specific host animal. While bird mites are specifically associated with birds, they will feed on humans in lieu of their primary hosts.


DISTRIBUTION & HABITAT. Bird mites are found in the nests of the host bird. If the bird is no longer in the nest, they will seek out alternative hosts. This is when they are commonly found inside the home.


IDENTIFICATION. Bird mites are barely visible to the naked eye, 0.7 to 1 mm in length. They are commonly compared in size to a period at the end of type print. They are translucent until they’ve had a blood meal, and then turn a reddish brown color.


LIFE CYCLE. Bird mites need to feed off their bird host animal prior to reproducing. Females lay several eggs at a time. They go through 5 life phases; egg, larva, protonymph, deutonymph and adult. Development takes an average of 5-12 days.


FEEDING BEHAVIOR. Bird mites feed on the blood of birds. In the absence of their host animal, they will feed on other warm blooded animals, including humans. Mites have specialized mouthparts they are able to insert into the host’s skin to remove blood.


HEALTH CONCERNS. The most common human reaction to bird mite bites are itching at the location of the bite.
