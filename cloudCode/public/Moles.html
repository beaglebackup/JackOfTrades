Moles

Class Mammalia

Order Insectivora

Family Talpidae


“Moles are notorious for the damage they cause to sod nurseries, golf courses, parks, cemeteries, residential lawns, ornamental planting beds and gardens as a result of their digging and tunneling in search of turfgrass insects and earthworms upon which to feed. The eastern mole, Scalopus aquaticus, is the most common and damaging mole encountered in these habitats.”


The Adult body length is 5-7 inches. Adult body weight is 2.5-4.5 ounces. The gestation period of moles is 28-42 days. The mole can have 1 litter per year of approximately 2-5 young (usually 3-4). The breeding & birthing seasons are February-March and March-April. The weaning age of a mole is 4 weeks. The activity period is anytime and the range is 0.2-0.5 acres. Insects, slugs, earthworms, small animals, seeds, roots are the primary foods of the mole.
